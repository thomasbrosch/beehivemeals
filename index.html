Metric Glossary

New-Customer CPA Watch this
What Beehive pays to acquire one new subscriber. Measured via Triple Attribution + Views (TA+V) — not what Meta or Google report. Target: $40–$55. Ceiling: $75. Kill threshold: 1.5× TA+V ROAS.
aMER Key metric
Acquired-customer Marketing Efficiency Ratio. New customer revenue ÷ total ad spend. The cleanest signal of whether new-customer acquisition is profitable. Prefer this over blended ROAS for paid media decisions.
Total ROAS Context only
All revenue (new + returning subscribers) ÷ spend. Looks good when subscribers are high but can mask acquisition problems. Use aMER for decisions — ROAS for context.
Cost per Session Efficiency
Ad spend ÷ sessions. How much you pay to get one person to the website. Rises as you scale into broader audiences. Blended target: ~$0.79–$1.10.
Conversion Rate Multiplier
% of sessions that result in a purchase. Small improvements here have outsized impact on CPA. Dedicated landing pages (e.g. Flat Top LP) run ~3× the CVR of homepage traffic.
New-Customer Share Mix
% of all purchasers who are first-time buyers. Higher = acquisition-heavy. Lower = mostly returning subscribers reordering. Watch this alongside CPA to understand what spend is actually buying.
AOV — Avg Order Value Revenue driver
Average dollars per order. Beehive blended AOV is ~$202 across Small/Medium/Large family tiers ($199/$239/$289). Higher AOV improves aMER without changing CPA.
TA+V Attribution Source of truth
Triple Attribution + Views — Beehive's authoritative attribution model via Triple Whale. Platform-reported CPA (Meta, Google) understates true new-customer cost by 2.5–3.4×. Never use platform numbers for budget decisions.

Goal mode uses Customers × CPA directly — no funnel math needed. Spend mode runs the full funnel (sessions × CR × new-customer share). Spend × CPA Matrix uses New Customers = Spend ÷ CPA — useful for scenario planning across a range of CPAs.
